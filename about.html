Early Life

Jarad Anthony Higgins was born on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois. He grew up in the South Suburbs of Chicago, initially in Calumet Park before moving to Homewood, where he attended Homewood-Flossmoor High School and graduated in 2017.

His parents divorced when he was three years old, leaving his mother, Carmella Wallace, to raise him and his older brother as a single parent. Higgins' father passed away in June 2019.

His mother was very religious and conservative and didn't allow him to listen to hip-hop. Instead, he was introduced to rock and pop music through video games like Tony Hawk's Pro Skater and Guitar Hero, exposing him to artists including Billy Idol, Blink-182, Black Sabbath, Fall Out Boy, Megadeth, and Panic! at the Disco.

Juice WRLD was musically gifted from a young age, learning to play the piano at four years old after being inspired by his mother. He later took up guitar and drums while also playing trumpet for his school band.

Music Career

2015

Early Beginnings

Began his career as an independent artist under the name JuicetheKidd, recording tracks on his cellphone and uploading them to SoundCloud.

2017

Breaking Through

Released his debut EP "9 9 9" and adopted the name Juice WRLD, inspired by the 1992 film "Juice" starring Tupac Shakur.

Released tracks "Lucid Dreams" and "All Girls Are The Same" on SoundCloud, which began to gain significant attention.

Signed a recording contract with Grade A Productions and Interscope Records for $3 million.

2018

Mainstream Success

Released "Lucid Dreams", which peaked at #2 on the Billboard Hot 100 and has since been certified Diamond.

Dropped his debut album "Goodbye & Good Riddance", which went triple platinum.

Released the collaborative mixtape "WRLD on Drugs" with Future.

2019

Rising Star

Released his second album "Death Race for Love", which became his first #1 on the Billboard 200.

Went on the "Death Race for Love Tour" and performed at major festivals including Rolling Loud.

2020-2023

Posthumous Releases

His first posthumous album "Legends Never Die" (2020) debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200, achieving the biggest first-week sales of 2020.

Additional posthumous albums include "Fighting Demons" (2021), which was accompanied by the HBO documentary "Into the Abyss".

His music continues to inspire a new generation of artists and resonates with millions of fans worldwide.

Musical Style & Influences

Juice WRLD emerged as a leading figure in the emo rap and SoundCloud rap genres, known for blending elements of hip-hop, trap, and alternative rock with emotionally raw lyrics.

His music often explored themes of mental health, substance abuse, heartbreak, and mortality with an honesty that resonated deeply with his fans.

Major Influences

  • Rock & Alternative: Billy Idol, Black Sabbath, Blink-182, Fall Out Boy, Panic! at the Disco
  • Hip-Hop: Kanye West (especially "808s & Heartbreak"), Future, Travis Scott
  • Rap Legends: Tupac Shakur, Eminem, Lil Wayne
  • Chicago Scene: Chief Keef and the drill movement

Juice WRLD was known for his impressive freestyle abilities, often creating songs spontaneously for hours at a time. His melodic approach to rap and emotional vulnerability helped push the boundaries of what hip-hop could express.

"I was singing 'Street Fighter' songs and stuff. The last song I made, I freestyled for 45 minutes." - Juice WRLD

Legacy & Impact

"Addiction knows no boundaries, and its impact goes beyond the person fighting it... We know that Jarad's legacy of love, joy and emotional honesty will live on."

- Carmella Wallace, Juice WRLD's mother

Musical Innovation

Juice WRLD was a pioneer in blending hip-hop with elements of rock and alternative music. His emotional vulnerability and raw lyricism helped push the boundaries of rap as an expressive medium.

Mental Health Awareness

His openness about anxiety, depression, and substance abuse created a space for fans to feel understood and less alone. He helped normalize conversations about mental health in hip-hop.

Live Free 999 Fund

Established by his mother after his passing, the Live Free 999 Fund supports programs that provide young people with access to positive avenues for processing emotions and affordable mental health services.

Commercial Impact

With over 30 billion global streams, Juice WRLD became one of the defining voices of his generation. His music continues to influence and resonate with fans worldwide years after his passing.

Personal Life & Struggles

Throughout his life, Juice WRLD struggled with substance abuse issues. He began drinking lean (a codeine-based mixture) in sixth grade and started using Percocet and Xanax in 2013. He was also a cigarette smoker before quitting in his last year of high school due to health concerns.

Juice WRLD was open about his battles with anxiety and depression, often using his music as an outlet to express these struggles. His honesty about mental health resonated deeply with fans who faced similar challenges.

He was in a relationship with Ally Lotti from 2018 until his death in 2019. She has remained a vocal advocate for his legacy since his passing.

On December 8, 2019, just six days after his 21st birthday, Juice WRLD suffered a seizure at Chicago's Midway International Airport and was pronounced dead at a nearby hospital. The Cook County Medical Examiner's Office determined his cause of death to be an accidental overdose of oxycodone and codeine.

The eerie connection between his lyrics in "Legends" — "What's the 27 Club? We ain't making it past 21" — and his actual death at age 21 has been noted by fans and media alike.
